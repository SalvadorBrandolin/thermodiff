    "Let’s analyze what happens when we have a summation involving index-based\n",
    "functions. Consider a term like:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\\sum_{k=1}^{N_c} n_k \\, \\phi_k \\, \\tau_{lk}\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where $\\phi_k$ and $\\tau_{lk}$ are index-based functions.\n",
    "\n",
    "For example, $\\phi_k$ could be a function of the mole number vector:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\\phi_k = f(\\mathbf{n})\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\n",
    "Something similar to the UNIQUAC terms (where $r_k$ is a constant parameter):\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\\phi_k = \\frac{r_k n_k}{\\sum_{l=1}^{N_c} n_l r_l}\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\n",
    "On the other hand, $\\tau_{lk}$ could be defined as:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\\tau_{lk} = g(T)\n",
    "$$\n",
    "\n",
    "Here, the indices $l$ and $k$ refer to binary interaction parameters between\n",
    "components $l$ and $k$.\n",
    "\n",

    "\n",

    "with respect to $\\mathbf{n}$ is zero. Because of this, we need to construct\n",
